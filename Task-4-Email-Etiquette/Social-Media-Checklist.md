# The College Student's Social Media Checklist        

## DO’S:  
* &#x20; **Audit Your Online Presence Regularly:**    Search your own name on Google to see what a potential employer or admissions officer might find. Delete any past posts that could be viewed as immature or inappropriate.

* &#x20; **Build a Professional Brand:**   
Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your academic achievements, internships, and skills. Follow industry leaders and organizations in your field to stay updated on trends.

* &#x20; **Adjust Privacy Settings:**    
Set your profiles to "private" so only people you know and trust can see your content. Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) to secure your accounts from unauthorized access.
* &#x20; **Post Meaningful and Positive Content:**    
Share articles, personal projects, or volunteer experiences that reflect your passions and well-rounded character. Engage in civil, thoughtful discussions rather than toxic arguments.
* &#x20; **Respect Others' Privacy:**    
Always ask for consent before posting photos or videos featuring your friends, classmates, or roommates.


## DON’TS:
* &#x20; **Don’t Vent About Your University or Professors:** 
Avoid posting complaints about faculty, staff, or school policies. Badmouthing your institution can suggest a lack of professional maturity to future employers.

* &#x20; **Don’t Share Sensitive Personal Information:**  
Never post your full home address, phone number, financial details, or your daily schedule. Be wary of sharing your exact location in real-time, as this can be used by stalkers or thieves.
* &#x20; **Don't let scrolling derail your focus.**  
Set app timers so social media doesn't eat up the time you need to prep for demanding exams like vector calculus or AI/ML.

* &#x20; **Don't post anything you wouldn't want an employer to see.**  
Unprofessional photos, illegal activities, or aggressive rants can easily come back to haunt you during background checks.

* &#x20; **Don’t Trust Privacy Settings Implicitly:**   
Remember that "private" doesn't mean "permanent". Anyone can take a screenshot or download your content and share it publicly without your knowledge.
